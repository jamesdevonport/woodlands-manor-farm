## Best Wild Swimming Locations – Cornwall

[**Bude Sea Pool**](https://www.budeseapool.org/): This natural rock pool offers a sheltered haven for a refreshing dip with stunning sea views. It’s perfect for families with young children or those new to wild swimming.

[**St Nectans Glen**](https://www.st-nectansglen.co.uk/): A hidden gem, this cascading waterfall and plunge pool is an adventurer’s paradise. Enjoy a scenic hike through the wooded valley before taking a dip in the crystal-clear waters.

[**Goldiggins Quarry**](https://cornishcompass.com/wild-swimming-goldiggins-quarry-cornwall/): This unique and dramatic spot features an abandoned quarry filled with turquoise water. Explore the surrounding cliffs and soak up the breathtaking scenery before a refreshing swim.

[**Golitha Falls**](https://www.wildswimming.co.uk/map/golitha-falls/): Another hidden gem, Golitha Falls offers a series of cascading waterfalls perfect for a dip or a paddle. Surrounded by lush greenery, it’s a tranquil spot to relax and reconnect with nature.

[**Respryn Bridge**](https://explorecornwall.uk/attractions/wild-swimming-at-respryn-bridge/): ake a dip in the crystal-clear River Fowey beneath the historic Respryn Bridge. This spot is ideal for a refreshing swim followed by a picnic on the scenic riverbank.

[**Westward Ho! Rock Sea Pool**](https://www.visitwestwardho.co.uk/Westward-Ho!-Rock-Sea-Pool): Great for families, this natural rock pool offers a safe and sheltered spot for a paddle or a swim. Explore the rock pools at low tide and discover a variety of marine life.

Remember, wild swimming comes with certain risks. Always check weather conditions, water depth, and tides before entering the water. Never swim alone and ensure you’re appropriately prepared.
