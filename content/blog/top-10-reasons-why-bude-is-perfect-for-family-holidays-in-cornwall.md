#### 1\. Beautiful Beaches for Every Occasion

Bude is home to some of Cornwall’s most spectacular beaches, offering plenty of options for families with children of all ages. [Summerleaze Beach,](https://www.visitbude.info/beaches/summerleaze-beach/) with its vast expanse of sand and gentle waves, is ideal for building sandcastles, paddling, and family picnics. [Crooklets Beach](https://www.visitbude.info/beaches/crooklets-beach/) is the next cove over and an easy walk at low tide. It’s worth the stroll, as there are two great cafes on the beach, [Rosies](https://rosieskitchen.co.uk/) and [Crooklets Beach Cafe](https://crookletsbeachcafe.co.uk/), with great views and accessible access.  Watch out for the summer events at Crooklets Beach Cafe, as they have outdoor music overlooking the sea most weekends. Let’s not forget there is a skate park at Crooklets Beach, too!

For families with adventurous older kids, Widemouth Bay offers excellent conditions for bodyboarding and surfing lessons and some great cafes for relaxing afterward. Don’t miss the hidden gem: The Beach House, which serves excellent food all year round and has a great beachside bar in the summer. 

Lastly, [Sandymouth](https://www.visitbude.info/beaches/sandymouth-beach/), [Duckpoo](https://www.nationaltrust.org.uk/visit/cornwall/duckpool)l, and [Northcott Mouth](https://www.nationaltrust.org.uk/visit/cornwall/northcott-mouth) are all owned by the National Trust and are all equally stunning, less busy, and a little further from the town center, making them a good option on days when you want a quieter pace during your family-friendly holiday Cornwall

**Note:** please be aware that Duckpool does not have lifeguards on the beach in summer, so as always, paddle unless you are a very experienced sea swimmer.

#### 2\. Great Wild Swimming Spots 

Wild swimming has significantly increased in popularity across the UK in recent years. According to the Outdoor Swimming Society, approximately 7.5 million people in the UK regularly swim in natural waters such as rivers, lakes, lidos, and seas.

The iconic [Bude Sea Poo](https://www.budeseapool.org/faqs/)l is a semi-natural swimming pool built into the rocks at Summerleaze. It provides a safe environment for children to enjoy the water all year round, and you will find many dedicated wild swimmers swimming up and down the 91-metre pool. There are many additional fabulous wild swimming spots nearby, and you can find our [top wild swimming blog here.](https://woodlandsmanorfarm.co.uk/wild-swimming-delights-near-woodlands-manor-farm/)

#### 3\. Outdoor Adventures in Nature

Of course, you cannot come to Bude without considering surfing. There are so many options for both the experienced and beginner level.

[Check out our recommendations on surf schools here.](https://woodlandsmanorfarm.co.uk/local-surf-schools-bude/)

For families who love the great outdoors, Bude is surrounded by stunning countryside and coastal paths. [The South West Coastal Path](https://www.southwestcoastpath.org.uk/) offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and opportunities to spot wildlife.

Families can also explore the [Bude Canal,](https://www.visitbude.info/exploring-the-bude-canal/) perfect for a relaxing walk or a paddleboarding adventure. The area’s many nature reserves, like the [Tamar Lakes](https://www.swlakestrust.org.uk/tamar), provide ample opportunities for birdwatching, paddleboarding, kayaking, sailing, fishing, and other activities. They also run the [local park](https://www.parkrun.org.uk/tamarlakes/) [at Tamar Lake](_wp_link_placeholder) on a Sunday for keen runners.

Coasteering is also offered [by Saltwater Safari](https://www.saltwatersafari.co.uk/), which we highly recommend for both surfing and other seaside adventures.

#### 4\. A Wealth of Family-Friendly Activities

Bude boasts many activities to keep families entertained throughout their stay, rain or shine. The town is home to charming cafes, local shops, and weekly markets (from easter to the end of summer) where you can pick up handmade souvenirs and excellent local produce.

[The Splash Leisure Pool](https://www.better.org.uk/leisure-centre/cornwall/bude-leisure-centre/swimming-bude-leisure-centre) is perfect for rainy days, featuring slides and wave machines to keep kids entertained for hours.  The Venue is right next door and offers bowling and four levels of soft play for hours of kids’ fun.  

[Bude Haven Recreation Ground](https://budehavenrec.com/) is a fabulous sporting facility right by the sea. It provides squash, tennis, bowling green, crazy golf, putting green, table tennis, and pool facilities to local people and holiday visitors at affordable prices.

If you hit a rainy day, consider the Rebel Cinema, which plays leading blockbusters and independent films at a very reasonable price.

There are some great adventure parks and activity centers nearby, such as [The Milky Way Adventure Park (](https://www.themilkyway.co.uk/) The Peters family’s favourite as they offer a fantastic range of activities for all ages) and [The Big Sheep](https://www.thebigsheep.co.uk/), which has the best animal shows, especially the sheep racing, the Ram show and the Duck herding demonstration, are a short drive away and offer fun-filled days out.

In the summer months, Bude even has its own [Monster Slip and Slide](https://monsterslipandslide.com/), a 360FT long slide that you slide down on your own or in doubles.

#### 5\. Fascinating History and Culture

Families can immerse themselves in the history and culture of Bude by visiting its local landmarks. The Bude Heritage Centre, located at [The Castle Bude](https://www.thecastlebude.co.uk/), provides a fascinating insight into the town’s maritime history and evolution. Kids will love learning about the area’s shipwrecks, smugglers, and Victorian seaside traditions. Additionally, families can explore World War II history through the [Bude Heritage Walk,](https://www.visitbude.info/walks-guides/bude-wwii-heritage-trail/) which highlights the area’s role during the war with fascinating landmarks and stories.

#### 8\. Year-Round Appeal

While Bude is particularly popular during summer, it’s a fantastic destination year-round. Spring and autumn are perfect for quieter family breaks, with mild weather ideal for exploring the outdoors. Even in winter, Bude’s dramatic coastline, cosy cafes, and festive events make it a charming getaway spot.

Ask any locals. Spring and late summer/autumn are fantastic times to visit Bude, and plenty is still happening. The shops and restaurants remain open, unlike some holiday destinations.

#### **9\. Dog-Friendly Destination** 

The simple answer is yes; Bude is exceptionally dog-friendly. The majority of cafes allow dogs inside but all outside, of course. And some great pubs welcome dogs too – our favorites are [The Bush](https://www.thebushinnmorwenstow.com/) and [The Preston Gate](https://prestongateinn.co.uk/).

The beaches have set rules for dogs at different times of the year. However, if you want your dog to run wild, there are many options outside peak times for the main beaches and all year round for the quieter beaches.

[Find out more about dog-friendly beaches here.](https://www.visitbude.info/dog-friendly-beaches-in-bude/) 

#### 10\. North Cornwall Location 

North Cornwall offers a stunning coastal escape with easy access to Cornwall and Devon. Just over the border in Devon, you will find the historic village of [Clovelly](https://www.clovelly.co.uk/) and the breathtaking landscapes of [Hartland](https://www.visitdevon.co.uk/northdevon/explore/villages-towns/hartland-peninsula/). Further into Cornwall, you will find the legendary [Tintagel](https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/places/tintagel-castle/) Castle, steeped in Arthurian myth. Food lovers will enjoy the vibrant culinary scene in [Padstow,](https://www.visitcornwall.com/destinations/padstow) home to renowned seafood restaurants. Surfers can also head to [Polzeath](https://www.visitcornwall.com/destinations/polzeath) for a nearby surfing location outside Bude. North Cornwall is the perfect location for exploring both North Devon and Cornwall, and it ticks all the boxes for every family member.
